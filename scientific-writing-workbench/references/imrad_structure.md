# IMRaD Structure

Use this reference to choose or adapt manuscript structure.

## Default manuscript shape

For original research papers, default to:

1. Title
2. Abstract
3. Introduction
4. Methods
5. Results
6. Discussion
7. Conclusion or final discussion paragraph
8. Declarations and back matter

## Section intents

- Introduction: establish context, summarize the literature gap, state the question or objective, and preview the contribution.
- Methods: explain design, materials, data sources, processing, statistical analysis, and reproducibility details.
- Results: report findings in a logical sequence tied to figures, tables, and analyses.
- Discussion: interpret the findings, compare with prior literature, address limitations, and explain implications.

## Alternative document types

### Review article

Use a synthetic structure such as:

1. Title
2. Abstract
3. Introduction
4. Search strategy or evidence collection approach
5. Thematic synthesis sections
6. Evidence gaps and limitations
7. Future directions
8. Conclusion

### Methods or protocol paper

Use a methods-forward structure:

1. Title
2. Abstract
3. Introduction and rationale
4. Materials and prerequisites
5. Procedure or workflow
6. Validation or benchmarking
7. Troubleshooting, constraints, and expected outputs
8. Data, code, ethics, and acknowledgements

### Short communication or brief report

- Compress the introduction and discussion.
- Merge methods into concise subsections if the venue allows.
- Prioritize the one or two central findings.

### Rebuttal or response letter

Use a reviewer-centered structure:

1. Opening summary
2. Comment-by-comment response blocks
3. Manuscript change log
4. Closing note

## Back matter defaults

Include only what is relevant and supported:

- limitations
- data availability
- code availability
- ethics approval or consent
- conflicts of interest
- funding
- acknowledgements
- author contributions if required by the venue

## Style rule

Outlines can use bullets. Final manuscript sections should normally be rewritten as prose paragraphs before delivery.
