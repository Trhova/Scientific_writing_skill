# Reporting Guidelines

Use this reference when the manuscript needs stronger reporting discipline or a venue-specific compliance pass.

## General checklist

- Research question or objective is explicit.
- Study design is named and described.
- Data sources, inclusion criteria, and exclusions are stated.
- Sample size or number of observations is reported where relevant.
- Variables, outcomes, and endpoints are defined.
- Statistical tests, models, and thresholds are identified.
- Software tools and versions are listed when they affect reproducibility.
- Limitations and potential biases are acknowledged.
- Data, code, ethics, funding, and conflict statements are included if needed.

## Reporting families to consider

Use the journal instructions first. If no journal-specific checklist is supplied, consider whether a discipline-standard family applies:

- CONSORT-style reporting for randomized trials
- STROBE-style reporting for observational studies
- PRISMA-style reporting for systematic reviews
- ARRIVE-style reporting for animal studies
- CARE-style reporting for case reports
- TRIPOD-style reporting for prediction model studies
- MIAME, MINSEQE, or related domain standards for omics datasets

Do not claim compliance unless the manuscript has actually been checked against the relevant items.

## Structured abstract guidance

Some venues require structured abstracts. Common headings include:

- Background
- Objective
- Methods
- Results
- Conclusions

If the target venue is unknown, keep the abstract unstructured unless the field strongly expects structure.

## Declaration blocks

Prepare these as separate short sections so they can be rearranged per venue:

- Data availability
- Code availability
- Ethics approval and consent
- Consent for publication
- Funding
- Conflicts of interest
- Author contributions
- Acknowledgements

## Figures and tables

- Make sure figure legends describe what is shown, units, sample sizes, and statistical notation.
- Tables should define abbreviations, denominators, and comparison groups.
- If a result is visually emphasized in a figure, it should be stated consistently in the results text.
