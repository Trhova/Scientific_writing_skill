# Peer Review Rubric

Use this reference for qualitative review or structured scoring.

## Review modes

### Qualitative review

Focus on:

- the central scientific question
- whether the literature framing is fair and current
- methodological reproducibility and fit-for-purpose design
- analysis and statistics
- results presentation
- writing clarity and internal consistency
- citation quality and claim alignment
- venue readiness

### Structured scoring

Score each dimension from 1 to 5 with a one-paragraph justification:

1. Problem framing
2. Literature grounding
3. Methodology
4. Analysis and statistics
5. Results presentation
6. Writing clarity
7. Citation quality
8. Submission readiness

Suggested interpretation:

- 1: major concerns that block confidence
- 2: substantial revision needed
- 3: acceptable foundation with clear weaknesses
- 4: strong with limited revision needs
- 5: unusually strong and submission-ready

## Review output template

Produce:

1. Executive assessment
2. Major issues
3. Minor issues
4. Rubric table or JSON
5. Revision priorities

## High-signal review questions

- Is the paper asking a precise question and answering that question directly?
- Are the cited sources appropriate for the claims being made?
- Could another researcher understand and repeat the method?
- Are the statistics or computational analyses described well enough to judge validity?
- Do the figures and tables support the narrative, or are they disconnected?
- Are limitations stated honestly, including what the evidence does not establish?

## Reviewer-response linkage

When moving from review to revision, keep a mapping with:

- reviewer comment
- response
- manuscript change made
- changed location
- unresolved follow-up if any
